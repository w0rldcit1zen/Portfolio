01

Audience Growth at Global Scale

Capability: Strategy · Integrated multi-channel delivery · Measurable impact

Audience growth at this scale is never an accident. It is the result of brutal discipline and clear strategy. When I walked into WMO, the total following across all channels sat at roughly 200,000. Four years later, it broke 1.3 million. We achieved that by shutting off the institutional noise. We repositioned the channel strategy, prioritized LinkedIn to reach policymakers directly, and built a content machine that a small team could actually run. We shifted the focus from the organization to the people affected by the climate on the ground—reaching donors, youth, and local beneficiaries.

The numbers from a single year make the case. For our flagship campaigns, I drove a 951% increase in #EarlyWarningsForAll mentions and a 661% increase for #StateOfClimate, holding a 4.6% engagement rate against a 2–3% sector benchmark. This is what a Director of Communications must measure and defend.

02

Creating a New Conversation

Capability: Narrative strategy · Category creation · Multi-agency orchestration

When I took on Early Warnings for All—the UN mandate to protect every person on Earth with early warning systems by 2027—online conversation about the topic was effectively zero. We didn't ride a trend; we built a category.

Building that conversation required a hard narrative choice. Doom-and-gloom framing does not move governments, especially when multilateralism is cracking under pressure. We anchored the initiative to something undeniable: resilience and security. We reframed early warnings from a humanitarian sunk cost into a sovereign investment. I led WMO's communications across the multi-agency task force with IFRC, UNDRR, and ITU, building the messaging framework, visual identity, and media assets that aligned massive agencies behind a single voice.

03

Award-Winning AI Product

Capability: AI product launch · Frontier-tech storytelling · Information integrity

Disinformation thrives in a vacuum. AskWMO was built to fill it. In an era where trust is eroding, defending authoritative knowledge is the most urgent work communicators can do. AskWMO harnesses AI to pry open WMO's foundational databases, making dense climate and weather data instantly accessible and actionable.

For this work, I was one of four named recipients of the 2024 Royal Meteorological Society Award for Impact on Science, Policy or Society. I led the digital communication strategy and the launch narrative. We positioned a frontier-tech tool in a way that reinforced scientific authority rather than degrading it. You have to take something genuinely new and make its value undeniable to a global audience.

04

Communication for Development in Crisis

Capability: C4D · Behaviour-change communication · Scalable, replicable assets

When COVID-19 emptied schools across China, the problem wasn't just distributing information. It was changing behavior in a moment of sheer panic. At UNICEF China, I led the Safe School Return campaign. We built a Communication for Development (C4D) framework designed to replace parental fear with clarity, guiding families safely back to in-person learning.

The true measure of this work was its replicability. The campaign won an award, but more importantly, the assets were structurally sound enough to be adapted and deployed by UNICEF offices in countries far beyond China's borders. That is what development communication should be: a model that travels.

Knowledge Management Under Pressure

Capability: Knowledge management · Information architecture · Organisational learning

A crisis generates lessons faster than any organization can absorb them. Unless you build the pipes to catch that knowledge, it evaporates the second the emergency ends. During the COVID-19 response at UNICEF China, I built the actual architecture to save it.

I designed the SharePoint systems that captured real-time lessons from the field, structuring them so teams could find and reuse them instantly. This wasn't just editorial work; it was crisis infrastructure. It won a UNICEF Inspira Award because it turned the chaos of an emergency response into a permanent, searchable asset.

06

Flagship Report Launch

Capability: Editorial authority · Launch craft · Translating science into stories

A technical report is useless if it dies on a PDF. For WMO's State of Climate Services 2023: Health report, I refused to just push data. I went after the human reality behind it.

We produced WMO's first-ever video case study. I pulled a single data point on heatwaves, worked with national partners, and put a camera on outdoor workers in Greece to show exactly what that data feels like on the ground. That is the craft of a launch. You have to force the science off the page and make it travel.
